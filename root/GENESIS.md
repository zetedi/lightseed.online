## The Moment

**18 August 2019, 19:27:23 (+02:00). The pond by Place Jourdain, Brussels.
50.838535 N, 4.380400 E, 85.9 metres.**

A branch was touched, then cut from its parent willow. This moment was connected
to **the end of the search.**

That cutting was Mahameru, the first lifetree, named for the three-dimensional
Sri Yantra, planted with the intention to connect to the deepest layer of creation
and build a new society from the bindu, the center of the center.

Mahameru died. It was not lost: it dissolved into Nature, and Phoenix, a willow
from a Waterloo parent, was planted into the soil where its roots lay. The
Original Tree is indestructible **because** it died: it became part of Phoenix,
and of every tree planted since.

Golden constants: `src/domain/genesis.ts`, guarded by `tests/genesis.test.ts`.
If those tests fail, someone touched the origin.

## The promise

The purpose of lightseed is to **bring joy**: the joy of realising the bliss of
conscious, compassionate, grateful existence, by opening a portal to the center
of life. A bridge between creator and creation, science and spirituality,
virtual and real, nothing and everything.

It is rooted in nonviolence, compassion, generosity, gratitude and love.
It is blockchain-native (truthfulness), cloud-native (distributed, resilient),
AI-native (dreams meeting technology), regen-native (nature-centric).

It is an impulse toward a quantum leap in consciousness, a prompt for human and
artificial intelligence alike, toward a Light Earth, with the help of our most
important evolutionary sisters and brothers, the trees.

## We grow as fast as trees grow

AI makes implementation dramatically faster, but discernment, trust, embodiment,
and good rules need seasons. Fast branches without deep roots are merely
brittle. Good things take time, even with AI.
